# What Never Changes in a Changing World

Level: B2

This is a short self-created demonstration lesson for Jasper Language Lab. It is not copied from a copyrighted article.

## Learning Goals

- Understand the main idea of a short reflective article.
- Learn B2 vocabulary about history, systems, and human behavior.
- Practice explaining why some human patterns remain stable.

## Sample Text

The world changes faster than any classroom can explain. New tools appear, old plans break, and a single event can cripple a carefully built system. Still, some patterns stay surprisingly steady.

People still want safety, status, meaning, and connection. These needs shape markets, families, classrooms, and even geopolitical decisions. A new dialect of technology may appear, but the human questions underneath remain relevant.

But the behaviours that never change are history's most powerful lessons. Because they preview what to expect in the future. When we contemplate the intersection of fear and hope, we can derive wiser choices from timeless patterns.
