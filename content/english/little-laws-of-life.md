## Summary

The lesson begins with a simple business example: even after a major economic crisis, Snickers remained a bestselling candy bar. This small example points to a larger idea. Technology, medicine, politics, and language can change quickly, but human patterns such as greed, jealousy, overconfidence, shortsightedness, and the desire for certainty keep returning. A wise reader studies not only what will change, but also what will stay the same.

## Grammar Focus

1. Rhetorical question  
   What will change, and what will remain the same?

2. Contrast: change vs. stability  
   Technology changes quickly, yet human behavior often repeats.

3. What-clause as a noun phrase  
   Readers should grasp what will remain the same.

## Important Sentences

- Change captures attention, but stable behavior teaches prediction.
- The better question is not only what will change, but what will not change.
- When a pattern appears in parallel across time, it may deserve our attention.
