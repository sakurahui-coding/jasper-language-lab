# Why Europe Wants to Tear Down Thousands of Its Dams

Level: B2

Topic: Environment and River Restoration

Source article: Rebecca Dzombak, National Geographic, "Why Europe wants to tear down thousands of its dams", published May 21, 2026.

Interactive lesson slug: `europe-dam-removal`

## Lesson Overview

This unit turns the National Geographic article and the user-provided GSAT reading handout into a public interactive learning unit. It focuses on topic prediction, article structure, context clues, vocabulary, cause-effect reading, inference, and author attitude.

The public GitHub Pages version uses a transformed summary and short learning sentences, not the full article text.

## Reading Strategy

- Use the title to predict the topic before checking unknown words.
- Build a topic map: old dams, river health, fish migration, water resources, climate pressure.
- Track the article structure: problem, example, policy, ecological reasons, opposition, conclusion.
- Answer GSAT-style questions by locating evidence instead of relying on memory.

## 5-6 Sentence Summary Template

- Europe wants to remove old dams because they can harm river ecosystems.
- Many dams are defunct or obsolete and no longer serve a useful purpose.
- They can block animal migration and create stagnant water.
- The European Union wants more rivers to flow freely by 2030.
- Although some people worry about cost, flooding, and change, supporters believe nature can recover.
- The key challenge is balancing healthy rivers with sustainable energy needs.
