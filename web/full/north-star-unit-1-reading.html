朗讀短文

Among the multitude of stars, Polaris is special because it stays almost fixed in the northern sky.

在眾多星星之中，北極星很特別，因為它幾乎固定在北方天空。

For a mariner, that permanence was more than beauty; it was a natural compass.

對水手而言，這種恆定不只是美麗，而是一個天然羅盤。

Before modern tools, sailors used the horizon and the North Star to estimate latitude.

在現代工具出現以前，水手利用地平線與北極星估算緯度。

If their equation was erroneous, they could err and lose the correct direction.

如果計算式錯了，他們可能會判斷失誤，失去正確方向。

Volatile storms could disrupt a journey, leave a ship stranded, and make a captain frantic.

變化劇烈的暴風雨可能中斷航程，使船受困，並讓船長慌亂。

When the clouds cleared, the sight of Polaris could soothe fear and alleviate confusion.

雲層散開時，看見北極星能安撫恐懼、減輕迷惘。
